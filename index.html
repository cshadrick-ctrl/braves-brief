A M M U N I T I O N
Then
A few thousand dollars was a story.
Now
A few thousand dollars is a Tuesday parlay.
Powerball trained the country to think in eight zeros. Crypto trained a generation to think in ten. Every week, a kid on TikTok screenshots a sportsbook payout that would have been life-changing money in 1995, and a fan in 2026 calls it lunch.
We didn't change. The fantasy did.

A M M U N I T I O N
The human insight
Nobody plays the lottery for the odds.
They play for the daydream.
08
A M M U N I T I O N
Powerball booms at $800M
and yawns at $40M.
Not because $40M wouldn't change a life. It would change almost any life in this country. But at $800M, the fantasy goes feral. The brain runs free. The mortgage is gone, the in-laws are taken care of, the boat is named, the speech is written.
At $40M, the brain stalls. The fantasy doesn't catch. The buying behaviour evaporates.
The daydream is the product. The dollar amount is just its container.

A M M U N I T I O N
And there's a second mechanic
People don't decide to play at the counter.
They decide on the way.
The Powerball player decides in the car. The sports bettor builds a parlay over coffee for a game that night. The daydream is fully formed long before money changes hands. The transaction is a closing motion, not a decision.

A M M U N I T I O N
Why the 50/50 underperforms
The Braves 50/50 fails on both counts.
i. The fantasy
The prize sits in the dead zone.
Too big to be a beer round. Too small to be a new life. There's nothing for the fan's brain to picture.
ii. The timing
The journey is four seconds long.
The customer journey has been compressed into a booth exchange during a game. Four seconds isn't enough time for a fantasy to form.

A M M U N I T I O N
This isn't a tagline.
It's a brief to the agency from itself. Every piece of work that comes from this should pass one test:
Did we make a Braves fan want something they didn't want before they read it, watched it, or stood next to it?
If yes, the work is doing its job.
If no, the work is wallpaper.

A M M U N I T I O N
What the work has to do
Five behavioural levers. All five, or none.
01
Move the decision upstream.
The 50/50 can't rely on a four-second booth moment to close a sale. The campaign does its emotional work hours before the fan arrives, so the decision is settled by first pitch.
02
Make the jackpot ambient.
The number is the most important asset in this product. Put it everywhere a Braves fan looks. Phone, podcast, feed, digital screens at The Battery, BravesVision ticker. Make it feel like the weather.
03
Build it social before transactional.
A fan who's told a friend they're playing has already bought. Create reasons to commit publicly. Group-chat-friendly content. "You in?" mechanics. Social pressure does the conversion, not the seller.
04
Make the stadium the payoff, not the pitch.
Every touchpoint from earlier in the day pays off in-park. BravesVision runs the climb. The activation around the winner is loud enough to recruit next week's buyers in real time.
05
Treat winners as the headline talent.
Real winners, real Atlanta, real lives changed. Their stories run constantly. They're the most credible proof the daydream is real - the thing that makes tomorrow's daydream real for the next fan.

A M M U N I T I O N
Tone
Confident. Vivid. A little loose.
A city that knows it's the South's biggest team and acts like it.
Not charitable. Not solemn. Not earnest in the careful way nonprofits often are. The 50/50 should feel like it belongs in the same conversation as a Powerball headline, a viral parlay, a kid stealing a base on national television.
The voice of the friend in the group chat who already knows the pot and is asking if you're in.

A M M U N I T I O N
Mandatories
  • The Foundation cause story stays tellable for stewardship and major donor work. It's the dividend, not the headline.
  • Harrah's Cherokee Casinos (presenting partner) is respected in the platform, not designed around.
  • The work runs on every surface a Braves fan touches. Phone, radio, social, broadcast, BravesVision, in-park, around The Battery, in-app.
What we're not doing
  • Not running player vs cause creative. That test has been run. Player won. We move forward, not refight it.
  • Not running impact-led acquisition. Stewardship work, yes. Acquisition, no.
  • Not making the booth the primary touchpoint. The booth is the close. It can't also be the campaign.

A M M U N I T I O N
What success looks like
Four behaviours. In order.
01
A fan decides to play before they reach the stadium.
02
A group chat shares the jackpot before first pitch.
03
A fan stays after the 8th inning. The winner reveal has become its own moment.
04
A fan in Macon, Mobile, or Tallahassee opens the app to check tonight's pot - on a night they're not at the game.
